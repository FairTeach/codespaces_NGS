### 🎯 **Module Overview**

The *Challenges in Biotechnology* module Personalised Medicine section, combines **advanced omics analysis**, **bioinformatics**, and **translational project design**.  
It aims to bridge the gap between experimental biotechnology, high-performance computing (HPC), and personalised medicine innovation.

Students will engage in **two major challenges**:

1. **NGS Hackathon (Challenge 1):**  
   A hands-on competition analysing Illumina next-generation sequencing data to achieve optimal alignment and quality control.
2. **Omics Innovation Project Proposal (Challenge 2):**  
   A research project proposal inspired by cutting-edge omics technologies, written in funding-application format.

By the end of the module, students will be able to:
- Work confidently in a Unix/HPC environment for omics analysis.  
- Apply critical reasoning to optimise NGS workflows.  
- Translate omics technologies into innovative project ideas.  
- Integrate biological, computational, and ethical reasoning in a professional scientific context.  
- Present and defend their ideas effectively, both in writing and orally.

### 🧩 **Tasks and Deliverables**

#### **Challenge 1 — NGS Hackathon**
- **Goal:** Align Illumina NGS reads using bioinformatics tools, improving alignment metrics and accuracy.
- **Deliverables:**  
  - Markdown/PDF report with code, results, and interpretation.  
  - Biological interpretation of data quality and mapping performance.
- **Evaluation:** Technical accuracy (40%), reproducibility (30%), interpretation (30%).

#### **Challenge 2 — Omics Innovation Project Proposal**
- **Goal:** Design a research project proposal applying one or several omics technologies to a novel problem in biotechnology or personalised medicine.
- **Deliverables:**  
  - Written report (10–20 pages) in Word or PDF format.  
  - Oral presentation (15-20 min, PowerPoint).  
  - Team submission.
- **Evaluation:** See below (grading criteria).

> 💡 A minimum score of **5** is required in each component to pass the module.

---

### 🧠 **Detailed Evaluation Rubric**

| Criterion | Excellent (9–10) | Good (7–8) | Satisfactory (5–6) | Poor (<5) |
|------------|-----------------|-------------|---------------------|------------|
| **Scientific understanding** | Deep, accurate, integrated biological & computational reasoning | Mostly correct with minor gaps | Partial understanding | Major conceptual errors |
| **Innovation & originality** | Highly original and creative idea | Moderate innovation | Derivative or limited novelty | Repetition of known concepts |
| **Feasibility & methods** | Clear, realistic methodology, technically detailed | Mostly sound but some missing details | Feasible but underdeveloped | Unrealistic or unclear |
| **Biological interpretation** | Strong and insightful integration of omics into biology | Good but lacks depth in discussion | Basic level of biological link | Weak or absent biological analysis |
| **Data visualisation / Figures** | Clear, professional, self-explanatory | Readable, minor design issues | Limited clarity | Poorly labelled or irrelevant |
| **Presentation / communication** | Engaging, concise, confident, well-structured | Mostly clear and coherent | Acceptable but uneven | Unstructured or unclear presentation |

### 🧭 **Student Guidance**

- Bring your own laptop for all sessions (Wi-Fi access required).  
- Ensure GitHub Education account is active (for Codespaces access).  
- Use Markdown to document all analyses and projects.  
- Teams will be formed during the **Introduction to HPC and UNIX session**.  
- Collaboration is encouraged, but all submissions must include **individual contributions** clearly stated.  
- Cite all external sources appropriately.

---

### 🧰 **Software and Resources**

- **Core tools:** FastQC, Cutadapt, Bowtie2, Samtools, MultiQC, GitHub Codespaces or Linux-based HPC access.  
- **Recommended editors:** Visual Studio Code with *Markdown All in One* and *Markdown PDF* extensions.  
- **Graphics:** BioRender, PowerPoint, or Figma for workflow diagrams.  
- **Reference managers:** Zotero or Mendeley.  
- **Optional datasets:** GEO, ENCODE, and Zenodo public omics repositories.

### 🧩 **Learning Outcomes**

By completing this module section, students will:
1. Understand the fundamentals of NGS data generation and analysis.  
2. Execute and interpret sequence alignment workflows.  
3. Design bioinformatics experiments using cloud and HPC environments.  
4. Integrate omics technologies into real-world biomedical or biotech contexts.  
5. Develop grant-writing, teamwork, and scientific communication skills.  
6. Critically evaluate omics methodologies and their translational potential.
